Engagement


Would the team recommend working here? Are the team enthusiastic when at work? Are the team able to make improvements happen?


Knowledge and skills are no longer enough and the route to success is through our behavior, how we lead our teams, how we engage our staff and empower them to take ownership and bring about improvements. We have a Leadership Framework in our organisation that has been designed to underpin and support the new vision, values and strategic direction for Lancashire Teaching Hospitals NHS Foundation Trust.


Value the individual to improve performance

Within your TED survey report there is an engagement score and this can be improved by improving how your team work together. With your TED team development journey you will focus on improving the key things that contribute to effective team working and by doing so these will improve your team engagement score.

This section gives an overview of staff engagement, the conditions that create engagement and some of things happening corporately with engagement.

At Lancashire Teaching Hospitals NHS Foundation Trust
engagement means:

Staff being fully committee to the organisation and their roles, having a voice, taking initiative and making a difference.

When staff are engaged they feel motivated and satisfied, they feel involved and they are proud of where they work. The Kings Fund (2015) explain that, “individuals who are committed to their organisations and involved in their roles are more likely to bring their heart and soul to work, to take the initiative, to the extra mile and collaborate effectively with others”.

A key factor of successful teams and organisations is the commitment to engaging and involving the workforce at all levels. There is lots of evidence that shows that engaged staff deliver better care.
