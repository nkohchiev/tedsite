Value the individual to improve performance

The Framework focuses on maximising performance and potential and builds on the Trust values. It captures and describes the attributes, behaviours and outcomes, which support people managers and leaders in improving their performance now and in the future, which will in turn improve the performance of LTH.

Emotional intelligence, helping the team manage stress and promoting health and wellbeing are also key to supporting individuals in the team.


Your team may really understand the technical parts of their role but high team performance is created through building strong relationships in the team and by creating trust and value between team members. This page looks at understanding your team members, motivation, and feedback.

Understanding your team

There are many things you can do to get to know the people in your team and you can also help your team understand each other. This section provides you with four tools: "Getting to know you", "Me at my best and worst", "One Page Profile" and "Motivators at Work".

One page profile

This is a tool you can use to develop understanding about team members, their positive qualities, how best to support them so they can reach their potential, and what is really important to them, which can improve motivation.


Click here to access “Getting Started with One Page profiles” and Click here click here to access the One Page Profile tool.


One page profiles can be used in team development sessions. One way of using them is to ask each team member to read out their profile, alternatively you could place the profiles around the room and ask invite the team to browse the profile. You could make it fun by having a quizzing the team afterwards to find out what they have learnt about each other.

Understand what motivates someone

It is helpful to think about what motivates the individuals within the team. There are some common theories about what motivates people. Read more about each theory and think about how you can use this information in your team.


The psychological contract refers to the relationship between an employer and the employee. Click here to read more and think about how it could help you to better understand individuals in the team. Research shows that job satisfaction and commitment are negatively impacted when employees believe that the psychological contract is broken.
