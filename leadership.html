Leadership


Is there leadership within the team that
provides direction and clarity? Does the leader
role model the organisational values?
Do individuals within the team feel able to raise
concerns with the team leader?


Knowledge and skills are no longer enough and the route to success is through our behavior, how we lead our teams, how we engage our staff and empower them to take ownership and bring about improvements. We have a Leadership Framework in our organisation that has been designed to underpin and support the new vision, values and strategic direction for Lancashire Teaching Hospitals NHS Foundation Trust.


Why is it important

The Framework focuses on maximising performance and potential and builds on the Trust values. It captures and describes the attributes, behaviours and outcomes, which support people managers and leaders in improving their performance now and in the future, which will in turn improve the performance of LTH.

What is leadership?

Leadership

vs

Management

Leadership

Doing the right things


  • Team Building
  • Consulting with the team
  • Giving responsibility to others
  • Being honest with people
  • Sharing a vision with team members
  • Inspiring others
  • Giving praise and constructive feedback
  • Resolving conflict
  • Thanking people
  • Nurturing and growing people
  • Listening

Management

Doing the right things


  • Reporting
  • Budgeting
  • Applying rules and policies
  • Discipline
  • Running meetings
  • Decision making
  • Communicating instructions
  • Assessing performance
  • Getting people to do things
  • Planning
  • Delegating

Collective leadership


Make use of the staff survey to have conversations with the team about what changes you can make to improve practice or develop staff.


Situational Leadership


Based on need for manager to adopt style relative to the situation, greater focus on the readiness of the team (Hersey, 1980)


Transactional leadership


A tool that helps you to think about improving how things are done and it could support improving communication within your team.

Providing direction and clarity

A leader’s role is to provide direction and clarity to the team. Part of this means translating the organisation’s big picture into team objectives and helping team members see where they fit.

Empowerment

Leaders empower the team to become leaders. Leadership is not just for those in senior roles. Organisations and teams are more effective when leadership is encouraged at every level. David Marquet(2012) states that empowerment means letting people bring their brains to work, it is about sharing power and redistributing authority. Empowerment means giving people the competence, confidence, freedom and resources (information) to act on their own judgments.

Coaching approach

Coaching is “based on the use of one-to one discussions to enhance an individual’s skills, knowledge or work performance…coaching targets high performance and improvement at work and usually focuses on specific skills and goals, although it may also have an impact on an individual’s personal attributes such as social interaction of confidence”. In our organisation we offer coaching courses to help staff develop their coaching skills and we also have a number of coaches working to support staff development. Contact the Leadership and OD team for more information.

Creating an environment where people feel able to raise concerns

Having an open reporting culture is key to the delivery of safe and compassionate care and staff need to feel confident and safe to speak up without any fear of repercussion or reprisal.

A Managers Guide to Raising Concerns

Your leadership style, how you communicate with the team, how you involve your team and your leadership behaviours can all make a difference to whether people feel they are able to raise concerns.
