Team
Communication


Does your team communicate effectively with each other to achieve the team task? Does your team make decisions effectively?


This section explores team communication and team decision making. Communication is an important aspect of any team. Effective communication can greatly improve the team’s outcomes. In addition people feel more engaged and motivated when communication is effective.


Select the buttons below to view supporting materials

Effective team communication occurs if the following are in place:

A safe climate which enables
people to participate
Team members to interact and
participate in communication
Good levels of
information sharing

Team communication is about sharing information, providing feedback and sharing ideas. It is a key feature of team effectiveness.

Effective team communication can help ensure there are no misunderstandings. It helps to organise the team and give clarity on work objectives. Effective communication can also improve confidence levels among team members to share ideas or raise concerns.

Do you hold regular and effective team meetings?

Effective teams meet regularly to share information, suggest ideas and review work. It is best practice to schedule a meeting every six weeks.

Team Huddles


Build time into the day for a 10 minute team huddle. You can use this chance to rally everyone for tasks ahead and give the team opportunity to ask questions or raise concerns.

Keep this meetings regular and people will start to see the value in them. The team will get to know that this is a time for discussion and come prepared. By having huddles you can resolve several issues at once.

Do you meet regularly with individual
members of the team?


Make sure you schedule time in with individual team members to meet with you on a one to one basis.

This provides you with an opportunity to discuss work progress, communicate relevant information and give them a chance to share ideas or provide feedback.

Develop opportunities to suggest ideas and provide feedback

Make sure you build in feedback mechanisms so that the team can generate ideas and solutions.

DEVELOP YOUR TEAM'S
EFFECTIVENESS AT MAKING DECISIONS

Often groups can make better decisions that one person operating on their own, this is because decisions often require considering lots of information and the most effective decisions are reached by fully exploring a variety of options.


Making team decisions can also help with team engagement because if people are involved in decision making this makes them feel more valued and they are more likely to understand the reasons about why decisions are made.

It is important that you involve the right people with decision making and you take the right approach to group decision making. This depends on a number of different factors, including:


  • Type of decision
  • Time and resources available
  • Nature of the task being work on
  • The environment the group wants to create
  • The amount of buy-in needed

Decision Matrix Grid

The Decision Matrix Grid is a tool that can help you make decisions as a team, Click here Having discussions as a team helps to build group understanding about why decisions have been made. This tool also helps you objectively consider your options and make appropriate decisions. This tool could also help you if you are leading change and want to involve the team in bringing about the change.

SWOT analysis

The SWOT analysis tool is an alternative technique to help inform your team’s decision making. You can lead a team discussion using the SWOT tool to understand your team’s strengths and weaknesses as well as the opportunities and threats your team faces.

Group Think

Sometimes teams slip into Group Think , this is where the desire for group cohesion and consensus prevents good decision making and problem solving, click here for a useful resource on how you can avoid group think and avoid fatal flaws in group decision making.
