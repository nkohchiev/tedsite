Working with
other teams


Which teams do you work with to achieve your team purpose? Do you work effectively with teams you depend upon?


It is important for the team to understand where it
fits within the organisation as this helps to build
team identity and purpose. Teams rarely
work in isolation and usually need to work with
other teams to achieve their goals. It is important for
teams to understand who they need to
work with and develop effective relationships with
other teams. This section provides you with tools
and resources to help build effective interteam working.


Select the buttons below to view supporting materials

Identify which teams you work with, review how well you work
together and identify actions

Team Network Map
Stakeholder Map
Stakeholder analysis

This tool helps you identify which teams you need to work with, consider the effectiveness of those relationships and identify actions for improvement

An alternative approach to the network map is to consider your stakeholders and there are two tools that can help you with this.

Inter-team working review

The Team Network Map, Stakeholder Map and Stakeholder Analysis provide the team the opportunity to review the effectiveness of interteam working. It may also be helpful for you to invite other teams to share their views on how you work together. A simple way of doing this is to simply ask them to answer the following questions:

What do you value about our
team when we work with you?

What could we do differently as
a team when we work with you?


You could ask the other team face-to-face, via email or invite them to complete an anonymous feedback survey.

World Cafe

This toolkit helps you to set up a World Cafe, it is a creative process which focuses on sharing knowledge, building understanding and improving inter-team working

Team Brand

This tool tool helps you to consider and develop a positive team brand.

The Perfect Shift

This is a tool that helps you to think about improving how things are done and involves identifying how you work with other teams.

Team Showcase

why not invite other teams to your team meeting to present a showcase of what their team does. This can build understanding and awareness of other teams and the potential for working together.

Top tips for
working with
other teams

Point 1


Don't forget noticeboards are quick and simple ways to share information.


Point 2


Why not set up a book within the work area where the team can log ideas? Make sure to review this and feedback to the team. It may be helpful to invite the team to log issues and also their ideas for improving the issue.
