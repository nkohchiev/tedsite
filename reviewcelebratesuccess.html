Review and
celebrate success


Does your team meet regularly to review goals and strategies? Does your team change plans when needed? As a team do you reward and recognise your successes?

REFLEXIVITY is one of the key areas that contributes to a real team

This is were the team meets regularly to review immediate goals and long term strategies and change these when needed

Teams often focus on the task at hand, and effective teams also take time to review goals and strategies, change and adapt when needed, and recognise success.

One of the best ways to improve performance is to provide individuals and the team with feedback and that is why it is important you take time to review team progress. It is an opportunity for you and the team to think about what is working and what is not working, and important clarify next steps

In addition, feeling valued and recognised
by the team, manager and the organisation
leads to higher engagement. It’s important
you take time to celebrate the successes
of the individuals and the team.


Monitoring and
review processes

Make sure you schedule one to one meetings with individual team members and as a team to review progress and plans.


The Follow Up Guide explores how you can follow up on actions agreed in meetings. Creating action plans and SMART objectives can help you monitor and review progress. These can be created for the team and individuals


You can use your action plans and SMART objectives plans in one to one meetings, team meetings or even schedule an annual away day where you can review plans



The documents below will help you to use the tool:



Annual review

It is useful to undertake an annual review as a team. Get the team involved either by holding a meeting or perhaps setting up an away day.
Structure your review by asking the following questions:

1.

What have we achieved?

2.

What have we done to contribute towards the achievement?

3.

Where is there still work to do?

4.

What has got in our way or what is getting in our way?

5.

What do we need to focus on over the next 12 months, or 3 years?

Top tip prepare
ahead of the
review

It may be useful for you to think about some of the answers to these questions ahead of the review meeting so that you can encourage the team and show that you have noticed the teams's performance successes.


Team away day agenda

You may also conduct a team away day to take stock of where the team are and the challenges that lie ahead.

lessons learnt review

Following success, an incident or completing a piece of work you may find it useful to think about the lessons learnt, which is the approach we want to take to create a continuous improvement culture. Organisations and teams that are successful focus on continuous improvement and by considering what went well and what you can do to improve.

The Personal Development and Performance Review (PDRP) is also an opportunity for you to review individual performance, achievement, contribution and development.

Celebrating Success

One of the key factors that influences whether staff feel engaged is feeling valued by their team, manager and organisation.

Often teams focus on things that are going wrong, but do you actively look for things that are going right?

How often do you show appreciation to the team?

How often do you say thank you or let the team know they’re doing a good job?

Team leaders need to create a culture where staff feel positive and engaged. There are three ways you could do this:


  • Formal recognition schemes such as internal annual awards, external awards and employee of the month schemes

  • Planned mechanisms such as having a monthly review of team successes, planning a day where you collect and share positive feedback, sharing best practice or case studies.

  • Less formal and more day- to-day interactions with your team where you give praise and recognition

Formal Recognition

Quality awards

these are the Trust annual awards. Are you encouraging your team so make nominations? Have you made a nomination for your team or individuals within your team?

External awards

there are lots of external awards and a comprehensive list of these is available on the Trust intranet

Employee of the month scheme

Why not set up an employee of the month scheme? Decide on what you want to recognise, for example is it someone who has gone the extra mile or perhaps someone who has really demonstrated the values? Let the team know how they can nominate someone for the employee of the month. Decide on how you will judge nominations, for example will you have a judging panel or will the manager simply pick the winner? Let the team know who has won employee of the month? Give them a certificate or a box of chocolates or find your way of saying well done

Planned mechanisms

Schedule team meetings where you can review and share team successes - Ideas on how to celebrate success.

Schedule a team away day or annual review and ask the following questions:

  • What have we done to contribute towards the achievement?
  • What have we done to contribute towards the achievement?
  • Where is there still work to do?
  • What has got in our way or what is getting in our way?
  • What do we need to focus on over the next 12 months, or 3 years?

Use appraisals to give someone praise and feedback on how they are doing

One to one meeting

Use one to one meetings with team members to give praise and feedback on how they are doing.

You can ask them questions such as:


What has gone well for you?

What are you proud of?
